Those who didn't vote

Nearly 65 percent of the voting-eligible population cast a ballot in 2020 Election â€” a figure that is the highest in over a century. So, there are still 35% of the population didn't vote. Moreover, there are greencard holders, non-immigrant aliens, like me, and undocumented residents that are not eligible to vote.

This visualization is an investigation into the population that didn't vote in 2020 Election. I want to shed light on this under-the-election-radar group of people, since we've already seen enough coverages on the other side. We are all going through it together, perhaps with different perspectives and beliefs.

The population of nonvoters is estimated as resident population subtracted by counts of total votes.

Data Source
Election Results: Tony MacGovern
Population: US Census Bureau, Annual Estimates of the Resident Population for Counties, 2019

* The data collection process is still a work in progress. There are non-resident aliens being left out of the equation; the resident population is US Census's latest estimation; and at this point we are still waiting for more mail-in ballots coming in.

* For the dataset is lack of election results in Alaska, the AK area appears grey on the map.

What is a day like in receiving 311 calls?

We all have complains. Might be an auto-renewed membership, or neighbors party music. While 'Contact Us' isnt always an option, 311 seems to be there 24/7.

The following visualization is an abstraction of our complaints to 311, by breaking down numbers of service requests filterd by complaint types and responsible agencies, using data pulled from NYC Open Data.

Analyzing complaints is a traverse. In August, we see the city-proof life routine - Noise complaints filed to NYPD has always came out on top during midnight, especially the weekend's; And reports of noncompliance with phased opening to the Mayor's remained constant during dining hours. Also, we see the aftermath of tropical storm Isaias - Complaints of damaged tree spiked on August 4, after the storm whipping through NYC.
